Amy Winehouse

Amy Jade Winehouse (14 September 1983 – 23 July 2011) was an English singer and songwriter. Amy Winehouse biography is a glimpse into the short and controversial life of Amy Jade Winehouse.She was born to a jewish family on 14th September, 1983 in the Southgate area of North London.

Amy was the youngest child of Janis Winehouse (Seaton) a pharmacist and her father Mitchell Winehouse a taxi driver. Amy’s only brother Alex is four years older than Amy.

Amy Winehouse is known for her powerful, deep soulful vocals and her eclectic mix of musical genres including R&B, soul and jazz.

The young singer's life took a turn over time with the weight of fame, and she began to struggle with bulemia, drugs and drinking. She didn't want to get treament because she did not feel like she was "some messed up person. Less than five years after “Rehab” was released, Amy died of alcohol poisoning.
